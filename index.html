About Me

My name is Shiva Omrani. I am a computer science BS candidate at the George Washington University, and I am planning on minoring in mathematics.

About My Work

I work as an undergraduate researcher at the George Washington University. My current research is about election integrity, and my advising professor is Dr. Poorvi Vora. My goal for this research is to mitigate the risk associated with computerized voting system by developing election audits to verify the announced election results. Dr. Vora and I have been working on developing audits with mathematical descriptions, which makes my work a theoretical computer science research.

For my research, I have been involved in the Election Verification Network (EVN). I attended the EVN annual conference, which took place on March 2018 in Miami, Florida.

During the fall 2018 semester, I have been a learning assistant for CS 2312 which is Discrete Structures II for computer science majors.
